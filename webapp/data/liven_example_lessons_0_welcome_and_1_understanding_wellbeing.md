## Pack — Begin Easy and Dream Big

Well-being has become quite a buzzword in recent years, don’t you think? It’s often seen as the ideal we all aspire to, especially when we talk about our hopes for the future or set goals for the year ahead.

As the Cambridge Dictionary defines it,

> Well-being is the state of feeling healthy and happy.

But what if we took this definition a step further? Imagine this state isn’t just a fleeting moment but a prolonged, rooted feeling of balance in your life.

Your well-being is about the continuous feeling of contentment and stability across all areas of your life. It’s deeply personal — it’s what works for you, and it’s knowing you’re safe, no matter what the world brings your way.

At its core, well-being is the harmony of three key dimensions of health.

## Physical

Our body is where our emotions are born, and we need to nurture it. Remaining happy requires fuel, and its absence makes anyone exhausted. Think about your best days—they likely follow a good night’s sleep, a balanced meal, and time for rest. When you make daily choices that allow you to nurture your body, you contribute to a prolonged stable physical health that forms the foundation for everything else in your life.

## Mental

Your thoughts shape your reality. They act as sunglasses through which you perceive everything. The more aware you are of your mental health, the sharper your mind becomes, giving you the ability to approach life with clarity and positivity. Your mental strength determines how well you adapt to challenges and how you cultivate optimism in the face of adversity.

## Emotional

Your emotional health influences how you navigate your feelings and accept your needs. Understanding your emotions brings maturity, stability, and healthier relationships. Well-being isn’t about constant happiness but about being aware of your feelings without judgment. Your emotions are therefore, a powerful part of who you are—let yourself feel them fully, we are precious that way!

## Not so baby steps toward the thriving you: What does it take?

Achieving well-being does not happen in one short flick of a finger. But you can build your life, step by step, with patience and resilience. Even when you make mistakes, know that you’re still moving forward.

That is why your habits have such a significant impact on being happy.

> Every thoughtful choice you make brings you closer to your well-being goal—and yes, you can enjoy the journey while improving your lifestyle

- **Sleep.** Aren’t you grumpy and exhausted after too little sleep? There’s nothing strange with that: sleep is the cornerstone of any other pursuits. It’s when your body repairs itself, and your brain processes emotions. A consistent sleep routine can enhance focus, mood, and energy.

- **Exercise.** Movement, any type that brings you joy, is an excellent way to balance your intellectual and emotional labor. Aside from the obvious benefits of strengthening your muscles, supporting your cardiovascular system, and reducing the risk of chronic illnesses, exercise is also a proven strategy that boosts mood and helps people productively express their emotions.

- **Leisure.** How you spend your free time actually matters a lot. Engaging in creative outlets, trying hands-on activities, reading a book, or pursuing a hobby makes you distinguish one day from the other and measure your progress in more than one way. It positively correlates with the feeling of autonomy and helps individuals develop a sense of meaning.

- **Nutrition.** A balanced diet is one of the critical aspects of your well-being because it, like sleep, allows you to heal physically and mentally. Even without an “ideal” meal plan, it’s possible to ensure that you receive the necessary nutrients to thrive. Moreover, it can also be a great mindful experience shared with loved ones.

- **Structure.** The way you manage your daily responsibilities—work, school, social commitments—affects your stress levels and productivity. By creating a flexible routine and understanding what to prioritize, you can transform your day and bring yourself closer to inner peace.

## In the heart of “us” or why close relationships matter

Relationships are the heartbeat of the human experience — remaining central to one’s well-being. Think back to some of the most important moments in your life. More often than not, there was someone there beside you—whether it was family, friends, a partner, or a colleague.

Strong relationships mirror our values and priorities, offering emotional support and motivating us to grow. Like a garden, our relationships require nurturing to help them flourish.

## How our relationships support well-being

We seek connection because it strengthens us; which is why we want people around us in times of need and even when we’re happy. The act of sharing our lives with others is precious and profoundly impacts our well-being. Studies show that even in the toughest situations, people feel happier and more resilient when connected to others. Strong social support can therefore, help individuals cope with extreme challenges, including economic stress, disasters, or illnesses:

> Feeling close to family and friends, or belonging to a supportive group, can ease feelings of isolation and alienation.

Having supportive relationships not only enhance emotional satisfaction but also provides a deep sense of security. Helping and supporting others can then lead to a sense of purpose that allows us to express love in healthy ways.

Research also shows that people with strong social networks live longer, healthier lives—whether they’re extroverts or introverts.

## Building healthy relationships

Healthy relationships require effort, communication, and time. All the parties have to actively contribute to maintain and nurture these connections, which involves respect, empathy, and mutual support.

However, many individuals haven’t learned how to identify people who are healthy for them or don’t know how to communicate their needs with those already there. Learning how to set boundaries and be vulnerable enough to let others in is key.

Don’t worry—we’ll guide you in finding your voice and becoming comfortable in your relationships.

## The tug-of-war: Short-term comfort vs. long-term health

One of the trickiest things about well-being is the need to balance between short-term comfort and long-term health. Trust us, we’ve all been there—sometimes it’s easier to indulge in instant gratification than focus on the long-term benefits. And that’s okay! You don’t have to be productive all the time. But it’s important to find the balance that moves you forward, not keeps you stuck.

## The appeal of short-term comfort

Short-term comfort often provides quick fixes to discomfort or stress. It’s tempting because it offers instant relief with little effort. For instance, turning to sugary snacks might give an energy spike, or binge-browsing social media may temporarily distract from a tough day. These habits are easy to fall into because they don’t require much effort and provide us with short-term relief.

While it’s completely fine, here are some signs that you might be leaning too heavily on these small comforts:

- you neglect your long-term goals
- you feel stressed all the time
- you can’t seem to stop yourself from this “comfort” even if you know you have to
- you regularly procrastinate.

These signs, henceforth, suggest that you may be neglecting tasks that deserve greater priority.

## Choosing long-term health

Building lasting health requires intention. It’s about making choices that may not feel rewarding right now but pay off in the future. You might be tempted to party three nights a week, but doing so could interfere with your health, relationships, and responsibilities. The key is balancing short-term desires with long-term benefits.

The lack of immediate gratification can be challenging, but as you begin to make mindful choices, you’ll see how small, consistent changes lead to significant benefits in the long run. The hack is to delay your instant gratification by saying “we will do this later” and see the magic!

> **Pro Tip:** A common mistake people make when trying to improve their well-being is trying to change everything at once by adapting the “I must” approach. But you can make it easy and — yes, really — fun.

Try this: imagine that you’re a video game character with limited resources — and while you have to progress from level to level, you have limited resources per day that you can spend between, let’s say, exercise, rest, mental health, healthy eating, and hobbies. Think about arranging these resources well without making it too complicated — a bonus point to do things that actually excite you.

Don’t want to go for a run? Then don’t. Try dancing, fencing, or tag with your friends (it’s a great exercise, by the way!). Don’t want to make healthy foods boring? Buy a fun cookbook based on your favorite movie.
